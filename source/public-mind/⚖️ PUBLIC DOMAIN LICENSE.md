- I like the version [Sunrise Oath](https://syllabus.leaflet.pub/3miog4d645s2r) wrote that contains this "anything is allowed to call itself ORI if it follows the ORI protocol". A self describing trademark:

```
## 

8. ORI Designation

A Work may be designated as part of the Open Research Institute ecosystem using the following form:

> “This work is adopted by the Open Research Institute (ORI) by virtue of being [released under the Open Research Institute (ORI) License].”

The bracketed clause may be replaced with any ORI-Compatible basis, including but not limited to:

- “released under the Open Research Institute (ORI) License”;
- “released under the Creative Commons Zero (CC0) license”;
- “in the public domain.”

Any Work satisfying the definition of an ORI Work may use this designation.
```

And I think this clause on attribution is good:

> Attribution must not be required as a condition for use of the Work by any ORI Work. Licensors may request attribution as a non-binding preference, but such requests have no legal effect within the ORI ecosystem.
